Arts For All

App redesign to create a more accessible donor friendly platform

Case Study

Challenge

Arts for All is a new New York City based NPO, Arts for All has the potential to reach it’s target audience of donors, volunteers, teachers and youths interested in affordable arts programs but the Arts For ALL desktop and mobile site needs a UI revamp that clearly communicates their value to all users.

Proposed Solutions

My amazing design team and I will build a clear and more effective webpage and mobile application with a UI that not only illuminates the business, but adds more guidance to different users to accomplish their goals within Arts For All non-profit organization. Stripping the website of cluter and creating a clear and cohesive theme will increase cause customer rentention.

Outcome

My design team and I created a Figma prototype that incrporates a better UX/UI for art enthusiast, including an improved navigation, layout, terminology, theme, and content quality.

Background and Research

Primary Research

In the Research stages of our Design process our team followed qualitative research and design thinking methodologies to compare and contrast the insights we gathered. As a result we completed over 8 user interviews based on user’s past experiences with non-profit organizations.

Interviews

As researchers, we needed to gather emotional insight. Our team wanted to get to know why users donated at all. What motivated them? What did they find compelling about a certain nonprofit? Most importantly, how the users justified their donation. Here are the examples of the questions we asked. Some Interview Question: What is your goal of participating a non-profit organization? How do you find new NPOs to support? How would you like to see your money used at NPOs you support? What are some of your favorite NPOs to support and why?

Competitive Analysis

With user insights from diverse persons my dedicated UX design team and I were ready to analyze some of the Non-profit sites mentioned by our interviewees. After visiting notable NPO’s sites and apps we created a comprehensive competitive analysis.

A common theme amongst the non-profits we combed through, they had a donate button that popped, and had a clear path to check-out. ‘Gofundme’ had the highest competition when gaining donations. They do not require the user to log-in. This makes it more likely a user will not back out of a donation if there are less steps involved.

Roadblocks

In our research stage my team and I noticed some roadblocks in the industry, many NPO websites are unable to provide users with specific programs of interest which result in users losing interest or donating less to the NPO, this may be a problem for Arts For All also since we can’t possibly provide all arts programs known to man for user's specific needs; It’s especially unheard of for a new NPO like Arts For All which is in need of exposure and funding. I utilized my problem solving skills and decided we need to add a sugggestion page within our mobile and desktop Arts For All applications which includes a description of how more donations will help Arts For All provide some of the unique programs suggested by donors and potential donors.

Research Summary

Through qualitative research practices, my team and I gathered insights that impacted our vision and future design for the Arts For All desktop and mobile applications. Ease of use, memorable website asethetics and compelling stories were on the top of our interviewees list of reasons why they donate and revist some NPO applications over other organization’s websites. Users frustrations with clutered web pages, unclear mission statements, and complicated navigations influenced our minimalistic design approach to Arts For All.

Persona and Journey Map

Based on the insights/data gathered, compared, and contrasted my deisgn team and I created a fitting user persona as inspiration for our Arts For All desktop and mobile applications, we refer to our user persona throughout our entire product development process.

For our “User Persona” we created our ideal Arts For All donor/supporter. The lovely Lydia Wang is a married middled aged mother of one and art enthusiast. Wang wants to share her love of the arts with her 8 year old daughter. Wang is always searching for affordable and enriching art programs for her daughter.

Empathy Map

Our Empathy Map highlights our user persona’s pains and gains and the reasons behind her actions, creating an empathy map helps my design team and I understand or target audience and design with a “user first” approah.

Site Map

The original Arts For All Website was hard to navigate and clutered with unnecessary information. My design team and I agreed one of the most important revamps needs to happen to the Arts for All’s confusing navigation, we created a site map to guide us as we design a brand new navigation for the site.

Sketches and Wireframes

My design team and I started the design process with unique sketches and low fidelity wireframes, setting a guide for our future prototype, this decision caused us to see what spaces should be in use and how we can simplify Arts For All's pages but amplify Art's For All's message.

UI Design

Clickable Prototype in Figma

After much revision and compromise on our wireframes and application ideas my design team and I designed a high fedility prototype that incorporates important user and organizational needs.
